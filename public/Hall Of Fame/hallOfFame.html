Ocean Conservationists Hall of Fame

Rachel Carson

An environmentalist and author of Silent Spring, a book that brought attention to the dangers of pesticides and pollution.

  • Her book, Silent Spring, published in 1962, was a major factor in the eventual ban of DDT and other harmful pesticides.
  • Carson's work sparked a nationwide environmental movement, leading to the establishment of the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).
  • She was a marine biologist by training and wrote extensively on the natural world, including the bestselling trilogy about the sea.
  • Her writing emphasized the interconnectedness of all living things and the impact of human activity on the environment.
  • Carson faced significant criticism and personal attacks from chemical companies, but she remained steadfast in her advocacy.
  • She is credited with changing the public's perception of the environment and encouraging a new wave of environmental consciousness.
  • Rachel Carson received the Presidential Medal of Freedom posthumously in 1980 for her contributions to environmental science.
  • Her meticulous research and clear writing style helped make complex scientific ideas accessible to the general public.
  • Carson's legacy continues to inspire environmental activists and policy-makers around the world.
  • She raised awareness about the balance of nature and the need for sustainable practices long before these concepts were widely accepted.

Sylvia Earle

A renowned marine biologist, oceanographer, and explorer.

  • First woman to serve as Chief Scientist of the U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA).
  • Known as "Her Deepness," she has led over 100 expeditions and spent more than 7,000 hours underwater, including setting a record for the deepest untethered dive by a woman.
  • Founded Mission Blue, an organization dedicated to establishing marine protected areas, or "Hope Spots," around the world.

Jacques-Yves Cousteau

A pioneering marine biologist, explorer, and filmmaker.

  • Developed the Aqua-Lung, an early scuba device, and produced numerous documentaries that raised awareness about ocean life and conservation.

David Attenborough

Sir David Frederick Attenborough is a British broadcaster, biologist, natural historian, and writer. He is best known for writing and presenting, in conjunction with the BBC Studios Natural History Unit, the nine nature documentary series forming the Life collection, a comprehensive survey of animal and plant life on Earth.

  • His work has inspired many to take action in ocean conservation.

James Cameron

Filmmaker and deep-sea explorer.

  • Directed Titanic (1997), which became the highest-grossing film of its time and won 11 Academy Awards, including Best Director.
  • Created the groundbreaking sci-fi film Avatar (2009), which became the highest-grossing film worldwide and introduced innovative 3D technology.
  • Known for pushing the boundaries of filmmaking technology, including pioneering the use of CGI and motion capture.
  • His work often explores themes of humanity’s relationship with nature and technology, as seen in films like The Terminator series and Abyss.
  • An avid deep-sea explorer, he completed the first solo dive to the bottom of the Mariana Trench, the deepest part of the world's oceans, in 2012.
  • Produced documentaries such as Aliens of the Deep and Ghosts of the Abyss, which explore underwater ecosystems and the Titanic wreck.
  • Co-founded the Avatar Alliance Foundation, which focuses on promoting environmental sustainability and addressing climate change.
  • Known for his perfectionism and demanding filmmaking style, often undertaking multiple roles such as writer, producer, and editor.
  • Advocates for veganism and sustainable living, promoting these lifestyles as ways to reduce environmental impact.
  • Continues to develop the Avatar sequels, which aim to explore new themes and expand on the universe created in the original film.

Enric Sala

A marine ecologist and National Geographic Explorer-in-Residence.

  • Founder of the Pristine Seas project, which aims to protect the last wild places in the ocean through exploration, research, and policy efforts.

Greta Thunberg

Although known primarily for her climate activism, Thunberg's work has indirectly contributed to ocean conservation by highlighting the impact of climate change on marine ecosystems.

  • Her advocacy has inspired a new generation to care about the health of our planet, including oceans.

Al Gore

A former U.S. Vice President and environmental advocate whose work focuses on climate change.Albert Arnold Gore Jr. (born March 31, 1948) is an American politician, businessman, and environmentalist who served as the 45th vice president of the United States from 1993 to 2001 under President Bill Clinton.

Jane Goodall

Primarily known for her work with chimpanzees, Jane Goodall has also been an advocate for ocean conservation, promoting sustainable fishing practices and marine protected areas.

  • Her work emphasizes the interconnectedness of all ecosystems, including oceans.
